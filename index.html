Hello

I am an Associate Professor of Decision Sciences at the University of Michigan-Dearborn College of Business, and currently serving as a Visiting Associate Professor at the Fisher College of Business, The Ohio State University.

My research focuses on operational strategies that influence firms' environmental performance and sustainability — particularly through operational transparency, information dissemination, and supply chain decision-making. I study questions in Corporate Social Responsibility, Modeling and Optimization, Quality, and Sustainability using empirical methods grounded in archival data.

I received my Ph.D. in Operations Management from the Georgia Institute of Technology, advised by Ravi Subramanian and Atalay Atasu. I also hold an M.Eng. in Manufacturing from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor and an MBA from the University of Warwick, England.

Before returning to academia, I spent over a decade in industry — as a consultant and solution manager at i2 Technologies (now Blue Yonder) across Dallas, Tokyo, and Japan, and as Director of Product Management at Servigistics (acquired by PTC) in Atlanta. That hands-on experience in supply chain management directly shapes my research and teaching interests.

I enjoy teaching quantitative courses that require both rigorous thinking and practical judgment — courses like Decision Analysis, where students tackle real-world uncertainty with structured analytical tools.
